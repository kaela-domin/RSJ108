How to Meal Prep and Save Time

Assembly Time: Ideally 15-30 minutes
Efficiency Rating: 200% Daily Efficiency Boost

Do you feel like you constantly run out of time to accomplish your errands or have no time for fun activities? By meal prepping you can fix an entire week (or more) of food in a short amount of time, allowing you to spend time on other things. Follow the steps and you'll always have food ready to go!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Glass or Plastic Food Containers
  • Baking Sheet
  • Parchment Paper
  • Mason Jars
  • Healthy Ingredients
  • A Chefly Attitude

  1. Select a day to dedicate to meal prepping each week.

    Meal prep can ultimately save you time and make your life easier, but you’ll need to make room for it on your schedule. Choose a day and time that works for you and block it out on your schedule as meal prep time.

  2. Write down a plan of action before you start cooking.

    Preparing several meals at once is a complicated process. To make the most of your time, write a list of everything you need to do before you start. Prioritize your list from the most time consuming and complicated tasks to the quickest and simplest.

    Healthily prepared meals are not just good for your body, they're good for your soul.

  3. Prepare bulk ingredients that you can assemble at mealtimes.

    Unless you’re preparing individual, ready-to-eat meals, you can make and store many of the components of your meals separately. Cook up large quantities of sides that you can split up between meals, or make a big batch of a main dish that you can mix and match with different sides throughout the week.

  4. Make sauces and dressings to use throughout the week.

    If you plan to use sauces and dressings, make and store them separately so you can use them whenever you need them. Set them aside so you can put them in their own containers until you’re ready to use them.

  5. Multitask while your meals are cooking.

    You can make the most of your prep time by preparing multiple items at once. Start cooking items that take a long time to cook but don’t require a lot of supervision, and use that cooking time to prepare or cook other items while you’re waiting.
